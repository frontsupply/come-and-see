The Savior finds people.

There are Saints who have read the Gospel of John carefully, set down the correlated manual, and met a Savior they had never been introduced to. We want to be honest about what we are sharing, and what we are not.

Two things are true at once, and we want to name both.

The first: thousands of Latter-day Saints have walked out of the Church over the last two centuries, many of them after reading the Bible carefully and finding a Jesus who did not match the one they were taught. Their experiences are real, and many of them are beautiful.

The second: most of the best-known ex-LDS testimonies on the internet route people into a different version of Christianity than the one this site is pointing toward. The authors have met Jesus, yes. They have also, usually, picked up a trinitarian framework that adds its own layer of philosophical formulation on top of what the Bible actually says. We do not want to send you from one extra-biblical system into another.

So this page is shorter than it could be. We would rather point you toward the Savior Himself, through His own words, than curate a long list of testimonies that would pull you in several theological directions at once. If you want to hear from people who have come out of the LDS Church into biblical Christianity as we understand it, a few of the ministries on our Resources page host those conversations. We will add named testimonies here over time as we verify both their experience and their doctrine.

The best testimony we can put in your hands is the Gospel of John. Everything else the Savior will say to you, He will say to you Himself.

A word on the testimonies you may find online

Discernment is a gift. Use it freely.

If you go looking for ex-LDS testimonies on YouTube, on podcasts, or in books, here are three quiet questions to carry with you.

Does the storyteller point you to Jesus in Scripture, or to a church? A testimony that ends I left the Church and joined X denomination is a different thing from a testimony that ends I opened the Bible and met the Savior, and He is enough. Both can be sincere. Only one is the gospel of Jesus.

Does the storyteller's doctrine of God match the Shema? Jesus Himself, when asked for the greatest commandment, quoted Deuteronomy 6:4. Hear, O Israel, the LORD our God, the LORD is one. When a teacher adds that the one God is actually three persons, or that Jesus is Himself God as well as the Son of God, they are adding to what the Scriptures themselves say. The apostles did not preach this. Gentle discernment is appropriate here.

Does the storyteller still hold to claims that caused harm inside the LDS Church? If a teacher left the LDS Church but still holds to a system where salvation is conditional on membership in the correct institution, or where ordinary believers cannot read the Bible without the mediation of a clergy class, the habit of mind that made the LDS Church feel safe has not been healed. It has only been relocated.

Voices we trust

Teachers who will not send you into another system.

Rather than a list of named individuals' ex-LDS testimonies, we are pointing you to a small set of ministries where biblical unitarian teachers (most of whom were not themselves LDS) do the careful work of explaining the gospel of the kingdom, the one God and Father, and the identity of Jesus the Messiah. If you are fresh out of the LDS Church, these voices will sound unfamiliar at first, because they are not the mainstream evangelical voices you would have been directed to by a well-meaning Baptist neighbor. Stay with them. They are holding on to what the apostles actually preached.

See our Resources page for the full list: Anthony Buzzard, Sean Finnegan's Restitutio podcast, J. Dan Gill's 21st Century Reformation, Spirit and Truth Fellowship International, Living Hope International Ministries, Restoration Fellowship, and the Church of God General Conference.

Your story is yours

We are not asking you to publish anything.

If you are somewhere on the path of faith transition out of the LDS Church, we are not asking you to announce anything. We are not asking you to confront anyone. We are not asking you to leave the Church today, tomorrow, or at any particular time.

We are asking you to read the Gospel of John with the Savior Himself as your guide. That is all. Everything else will follow in its own time, in the way that is right for you and your family.
