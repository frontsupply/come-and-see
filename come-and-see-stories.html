Stories

They came to see.

Returned missionaries. BYU faculty. Lifelong members born in the covenant. Temple workers who had memorized every ordinance. These are a few of the people who left, not out of bitterness, but because somewhere along the way they met the Jesus of the New Testament, and could not un-meet Him.

Every story here belongs to someone who spent years, often decades, inside the Church. Most were BIC. Most served missions. Several raised their children in the gospel. None of them left easily. And each of them, at some point, read the Bible with fresh eyes and found a Savior they had not been introduced to before.

We are not linking their stories because we agree with everything they say. We are linking them because they know what the shelf feels like, and because they have written their experiences carefully, and because hearing from someone who has been where you are is often the first step toward knowing you are not alone.

A word before the stories

The Savior is the one who never fails.

Some of the best-known ex-LDS voices of the last decade have, more recently, fallen into public moral failure. We think it is important to name this plainly on a page like this one. Ministries end. Pastors sin. Authors stumble. This is not cause for despair, because our trust was never in the messengers. It was in the message. The Savior is the one who never fails. The voices you read below are included because their testimonies remain fruitful, their lives bear up under scrutiny, and their ministries still stand.

If a name you were expecting to see here is not on the list, that may be why. We have been careful on purpose. This is a pastoral resource, and we owe you care on the far side of the search bar.

Former BYU professor

A tenured professor who knew the doctrine cold.

Book | Former BYU faculty | Active ministry

Lynn Wilder, Unveiling Grace

Tenured faculty at Brigham Young University. Convert in 1977. Temple-endowed for nearly three decades. Now co-leads the ministry Ex-Mormon Christians United for Jesus with her husband Michael.

The Wilder family joined the LDS Church in 1977 after missionaries knocked on their door in Indiana. All three of their sons served missions. Lynn eventually became a tenured professor at BYU, the kind of Latter-day Saint whose testimony was the center of the family's life. When one of her sons came home from his mission and told her what he had found in the New Testament, she did not accept it easily. She studied. She fought. She prayed. And she read the Gospel of John, slowly, as if for the first time. Her book is the careful story of the Wilders' transition as a family, told with love for the Saints and no bitterness.

"The doctrines I had taught, the covenants I had made, the ordinances I had performed, had never once introduced me to the Savior I met in the Gospel of John. When I finally met Him, I understood what I had been missing for thirty years."

Temple workers

Those who had learned every sign and every token.

Ministry | Former temple Mormon | Four decades of work

Sandra Tanner, lifelong witness

Great-great-granddaughter of Brigham Young. More than sixty years of careful, primary-source scholarship on Mormon history.

Sandra Tanner grew up in the heart of Salt Lake City Mormonism, a direct descendant of Brigham Young. With her late husband Jerald, she spent decades quietly publishing primary-source documents from Church history, always from the Church's own archives, always with citations. Her ministry was founded not to tear down but to tell the truth as carefully as possible. Many of the historical points you have read on this site trace back to research she and Jerald did long before the Church's Gospel Topics essays existed.

"We never had to make anything up. The Church's own documents were more than enough. What we did was put them in front of people who had never been shown them."

Born in the covenant

The ones who never knew anything else.

Podcast and conversations | Former BIC members

Faith After Mormonism conversations

Long-form interviews with former Latter-day Saints who have found Jesus in historic Christianity.

Faith After Mormonism is a podcast and ministry focused specifically on the transition from Mormonism to biblical Christianity. Most of the voices are former BIC members, many of them leaders, who describe their transitions in their own words. The posture is pastoral, not polemic. The interviews are long enough that you can hear the real texture of a faith transition, not just the bullet points.

"I was told my whole life that the church outside the Church was spiritually dead. What I found, when I finally walked across the parking lot, was the opposite. They had been worshiping Jesus all along."

Your story is yours

We are not asking you to publish anything.

These stories exist because the people who told them wanted them told. Most Saints who leave the Church do not want a platform. They want peace, and family, and a quiet place to worship. If you are reading this and you are somewhere on the same path, we are not asking you to announce anything. We are not asking you to confront anyone. We are not asking you to leave the Church today, tomorrow, or at any particular time.

We are asking you to read the Gospel of John with the Savior Himself as your guide. That is all. Everything else will follow in its own time, in the way that is right for you and your family.
