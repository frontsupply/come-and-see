Resources

Books, podcasts, first sources.

A curated, not exhaustive, reading list for anyone who wants to dig deeper. Grouped by what it is and who it's best for, with a note at the top on what to read first.

If you read nothing else on this list, read the Gospel of John. We mean that. Nothing replaces it. The rest of what follows is useful, and some of it is excellent, but the Gospel of John is the one book that has to come first.

A note on what is here

We have tried to include resources from a range of voices. Some are written by former Latter-day Saints who have come to biblical Christianity and want to help others on the same path. Some are primary-source historical documents, including the Church's own publications. A few are secular or academic and are included because they are careful with the record. We do not endorse every claim or posture in every resource, but each one is here because it is serious, well-sourced, and treats members of the Church with respect.

Where you should start depends on where you are. If you are feeling the shelf shake, read Lynn Wilder first. If you are ready for a careful comparison of what the Church and historic Christianity each teach, read McKeever and Johnson's Mormonism 101. If you want primary sources, read the Gospel Topics essays on the Church's own website and the Joseph Smith Papers.

Books

Personal testimonies and careful scholarship.

Start here | Comprehensive reference | In print

Mormonism 101

Bill McKeever and Eric Johnson, Baker Books, 2015 (revised edition)

Co-authored by the founder of Mormonism Research Ministry, this is a careful, gentle, thorough handbook on the doctrinal differences between historic Christianity and the LDS Church. Written for Christians who want to understand what their Latter-day Saint friends actually believe, and for members curious about what is outside their tradition. Accessible, well-sourced, and respectful throughout.

Practical | For members and for friends of members

Introducing Christianity to Mormons

Bill McKeever and Eric Johnson, Harvest House, 2022

The companion volume to Mormonism 101. Where 101 explains what Mormonism teaches, this book focuses on what Christianity teaches, framed for a Latter-day Saint reader. If you are a member who has grown curious about what Christians actually believe, this book is specifically written for you.

Memoir | Former BYU professor | Active ministry

Unveiling Grace

Lynn Wilder, Zondervan, 2013

Written by a tenured BYU professor whose entire family transitioned from deep LDS involvement to biblical Christianity. Careful, literate, and honest about how hard it was. Lynn and her husband Michael now run the ministry Ex-Mormon Christians United for Jesus. One of the best books ever written on what it is like to leave.

Comprehensive | Theological comparison

The Kingdom of the Cults

Walter Martin, originally 1965, multiple updated editions

Long the standard evangelical reference work on theological differences between LDS teaching and historic Christianity. Older in places, occasionally polemic in tone, but encyclopedic in scope and useful as a starting reference. The Mormonism chapter is a good overview of the core doctrinal questions. Best used alongside the softer pastoral books, not in place of them.

Historical scholarship | Secular biography

No Man Knows My History

Fawn Brodie, 1945 (revised 1971)

The classic scholarly biography of Joseph Smith, written by a BYU-raised historian who left the Church. Brodie is honest about both the charismatic genius of Joseph Smith and the troubling pattern of his prophetic claims. Not pastoral in tone, and dated in places, but the foundational academic treatment of the man.

Historical scholarship | LDS scholar, critical

Mormon Hierarchy series

D. Michael Quinn, Signature Books, 1994 to 2002

Three volumes of scholarly history by one of the most respected LDS historians of his generation, drawing on archival research. Quinn was later excommunicated by the Church for refusing to stop publishing. The series is dense but invaluable for understanding the internal history the correlated materials tend to leave out.

Primary sources

Read the record for yourself.

Official | The Church's own scholarship

The Joseph Smith Papers Project

Published by the Church Historian's Press

An ongoing documentary editing project that is publishing every known document written by or to Joseph Smith, including journals, letters, revelations, and discourses. Primary sources, transcribed, annotated, and freely available online. This is the Church's own scholarship. If you want to know what the Prophet actually said and wrote, start here.

josephsmithpapers.org →

Official | Church transparency initiative

Gospel Topics Essays

Published on ChurchofJesusChrist.org, 2013 to 2015

Thirteen essays commissioned under the direction of the Brethren to address specific historical questions the Church had previously handled less openly: the Book of Abraham translation, polygamy in Nauvoo and Kirtland, race and the priesthood, the First Vision accounts, the nature of God, the Mountain Meadows Massacre, and others. Cited throughout this site. The essays are short, carefully written, and worth reading in full.

churchofjesuschrist.org/gospel-topics-essays →

Academic | Scholarly journal on Mormon thought

Dialogue: A Journal of Mormon Thought

Independent scholarly quarterly since 1966

Independent journal where faithful and questioning LDS scholars have worked through difficult questions for over half a century. Not hostile to the Church. Not controlled by the Church. Back issues include some of the most careful writing on the Masonic connections to the temple endowment, the polygamy question, the historicity of the Book of Mormon, and more.

dialoguejournal.com →
Podcasts & video

Hear it in the voice of someone who has been there.

Podcast | Pastoral | Former LDS

Faith After Mormonism

Hosted by Bryan Hall

A pastoral podcast focused specifically on the transition from LDS membership to biblical Christianity. Interviews with former members, conversations about specific doctrinal questions, and an overall tone of care for people in the middle of a faith transition. One of the warmest resources in this space.

faithaftermormonism.org →

Ministry | Since 1979 | Daily radio program

Mormonism Research Ministry

Bill McKeever and Eric Johnson, Draper, Utah

Founded in 1979 by Bill McKeever, MRM has been quietly producing careful, well-sourced content for more than four decades. Their daily radio program Viewpoint on Mormonism has aired since 2011, with over three thousand episodes archived on their site. Their blog Mormonism in the News, written by Sharon Lindbloom, tracks current events inside the Church with calm, clear analysis. MRM operates the Utah Christian Research Center in Draper, which is open to visitors four days a week.

mrm.org →

Ministry | Former BYU faculty | Active

Ex-Mormon Christians United for Jesus

Michael and Lynn Wilder, founders

Founded by Mike and Lynn Wilder, who joined the LDS Church in 1977 and came out to biblical Christianity after thirty years, ECUJ produces teaching videos, testimony material, and resources for Christians who want to understand and love Latter-day Saints well. Also publishes at unveilingmormonism.com. A warm, pastoral ministry that treats the Saints with love.

unveilingmormonism.com →

Ministry | Long-term primary-source research

Utah Lighthouse Ministry

Sandra Tanner, Salt Lake City

Founded in 1983 by Jerald and Sandra Tanner (Sandra is the great-great-granddaughter of Brigham Young). The Tanners spent decades publishing primary-source documents from Church history, always carefully cited and always calm in tone. Much of the evidence now acknowledged in the Gospel Topics essays was first put into wide circulation by their work.

utlm.org →

Podcast | General ex-Mormon, secular-leaning

Mormon Stories

John Dehlin

A more general-audience podcast covering the full range of experiences of people leaving the Church, including many who leave for reasons other than biblical Christianity. Useful for the breadth of testimony and the depth of historical interviews. Note that the host is not a Christian, so the reasons people give for leaving are not always the reasons we would emphasize. Read with discernment, as with any resource.

mormonstories.org →
For the Bible itself

When you are ready for a whole Bible, here is how to start.

Translation | Readable, faithful to the Greek and Hebrew

The ESV Study Bible or the CSB

English Standard Version or Christian Standard Bible

You already have the King James Version, and it is a fine translation in the hands of a reader willing to work with 400-year-old English. If you want a translation that is easier to read while still being careful with the original languages, the ESV and the CSB are both good choices. A study Bible with footnotes and cross-references can help when a passage is confusing. You are not betraying anyone by reading a different translation.

Devotional | For anyone trying to meet Jesus

Mere Christianity

C. S. Lewis, 1952

The most widely read introduction to mere historic Christianity in English. Lewis was a literature professor at Oxford who came to faith late in life and wrote about it with unusual clarity. Good for someone who wants to understand what Christians outside the Latter-day Saint tradition actually believe, explained by a careful thinker.

Devotional | Pastoral on the cross and resurrection

The Cross of Christ

John Stott, InterVarsity Press, 1986

A deep but accessible book on what the Savior accomplished at the cross. Stott writes like a pastor, with care for the reader, and with the whole Bible in view. If you want to understand what grace means in the biblical sense, as Paul describes it, this is a good companion.

Still just one place to start.

All of this can wait. The Gospel of John cannot. A seven-day guided reading plan, with no commentary and no manual. Just the Savior in His own words.
