Resources

Teachers we trust.

A curated, not exhaustive, list of ministries, books, podcasts, and primary sources. Every one of them holds to the one God (the Father), to Jesus as His appointed Son and Messiah, and to the Scriptures as sufficient for faith and life.

If you read nothing else on this list, read the Gospel of John. Nothing replaces it. Everything else here is useful, but the Gospel of John is the one book that has to come first.

A note on what is here, and why

Much of the Christian writing available in English today assumes the fourth-century creedal formulations about the nature of God as settled ground. We believe those formulations went beyond what Scripture itself teaches. The resources on this page are chosen because they hold to biblical Christianity: one God, the Father, who sent His Son Jesus the Messiah, raised Him from the dead, and is bringing forth His kingdom. Some are described as biblical unitarian, some as restorationist, some as the Church of God heritage. What they share is a commitment to let the Bible itself, rather than later creeds, define the faith.

If you are coming out of the LDS Church, or looking into it, you deserve better than to leave one unbiblical system for another. These resources are the ones we would hand a family member.

Books

Biblical Christianity, carefully argued.

Start here | Comprehensive | Biblical unitarian

Jesus Was Not a Trinitarian

Anthony Buzzard, Restoration Fellowship, 2007

A careful, thorough, eminently readable case for the biblical unitarian position, written by one of the most prolific teachers of the position in English. Dr. Buzzard taught at Atlanta Bible College (a Church of God General Conference institution) for decades and has written more than a dozen books on the gospel of the kingdom and the identity of the Messiah. This book is the best single starting point.

Foundational | Co-authored with Charles Hunting

The Doctrine of the Trinity: Christianity's Self-Inflicted Wound

Anthony Buzzard and Charles Hunting, International Scholars Publications, 1998

A longer, more academic case for the biblical unitarian reading, tracing the historical development of Trinitarian doctrine from the pre-Nicene period through the post-Constantinian councils. Valuable for readers who want the full historical picture of how creedal Christianity came to its current form.

Gospel of the kingdom | For the whole Bible story

Our Fathers Who Aren't in Heaven

Anthony Buzzard, Restoration Fellowship

A sustained treatment of the gospel of the kingdom, which Buzzard argues is the gospel Jesus preached and the gospel the apostles preached after Him. The book makes the case that much of modern Christianity has quietly substituted a different gospel (often focused on going to heaven when we die) for the one the Bible actually teaches.

Compiled essays | Multiple contributors

The One God, the Father, One Man Messiah Translation (commentary volume)

21st Century Reformation Press

A resource volume accompanying the One God translation project, with commentary essays on key Christological texts by a range of biblical unitarian teachers. A useful reference for anyone working through the standard trinitarian proof-texts (John 1:1, Philippians 2, Colossians 1, Hebrews 1) and wanting careful biblical unitarian exegesis.

Podcasts and audio teaching

Voices worth listening to weekly.

Podcast | Sean Finnegan | Multiple episodes weekly

Restitutio

Sean Finnegan, host. Living Hope International Ministries.

One of the most careful, charitable, and well-researched podcasts on biblical unitarian theology and the historic gospel of the kingdom. Sean teaches through specific doctrinal questions, interviews scholars and pastors from across the restorationist world, and hosts extended conversations on church history, Christology, and the gospel. The archive is deep. New listeners can start anywhere, but the topical series on the Trinity, the deity of Christ, and the kingdom of God are especially good.

restitutio.org

Teaching ministry | Anthony Buzzard | Long-running

Focus on the Kingdom

Anthony Buzzard and the team at Restoration Fellowship

A monthly magazine and online teaching ministry that has been in continuous publication for decades. The articles are short, accessible, and consistently pointed back to Scripture. A good resource for anyone wanting a steady stream of biblical unitarian teaching grounded in the gospel of the kingdom.

focusonthekingdom.org

Video and teaching | Long-form study

21st Century Reformation

J. Dan Gill and colleagues

A teaching and publishing ministry focused on making biblical unitarian theology accessible to a general audience. Their video presentations cover the identity of God, the identity of Jesus, the gospel of the kingdom, and the biblical hope of the resurrection, always with care to work from the text of Scripture rather than from later creedal formulations.

21stcr.org
Ministries and fellowships

Places to belong, or to point a friend.

Fellowship of churches | United States | Since 1921

Church of God General Conference

Also called Church of God of the Abrahamic Faith. Headquarters in Morrow, Georgia.

A fellowship of biblical unitarian churches across the United States and Canada, with roots in the nineteenth-century Restoration Movement. COGGC holds to the one God (the Father), to Jesus as His Son and Messiah, to the Bible alone as the rule of faith, to believer baptism by immersion, and to the hope of the kingdom of God on earth. Their affiliated school, Atlanta Bible College, has trained biblical unitarian pastors and teachers for generations. If you are looking for a local fellowship that holds to these doctrines, their church directory is a good place to start.

coggc.org

Ministry | Research, publishing, teaching

Spirit & Truth Fellowship International

J. Dan Gill and team. Indianapolis, Indiana.

A ministry producing careful biblical teaching resources, conferences, and the Revised English Version (REV) Bible translation, which is translated from the original languages by biblical unitarian scholars without the trinitarian assumptions that shape most modern translations. Their commentary notes and articles are a serious scholarly resource for anyone studying Christology, the nature of God, or the biblical gospel.

stfonline.org

Ministry | Media and teaching | Atlanta, Georgia

Restoration Fellowship

Founded by Anthony Buzzard

The publishing and teaching ministry associated with Anthony Buzzard's decades of work. Their library includes books, articles, videos, and study materials on the gospel of the kingdom, the identity of God and Messiah, and the hope of the resurrection. A deep archive for anyone new to biblical unitarian teaching.

restorationfellowship.org

Ministry | Teaching and conferences

Living Hope International Ministries

Based in upstate New York

A teaching ministry that hosts the annual Theological Conference (a long-running gathering of biblical unitarian pastors, scholars, and teachers), and produces the Restitutio podcast. Their conference messages, freely available online, are one of the best ways to hear biblical unitarian theology preached and taught by a range of voices.

livinghopeiminc.com
Primary sources on LDS history

The receipts from the Church's own record.

These are research resources, not doctrinal teachers. Use them for verifying the historical claims made on this site and for going deeper into the documentary record. The views of the individuals and ministries below on the nature of God and the gospel are not necessarily ours; we list them only for their careful historical work.

Official | The Church's own scholarship

The Joseph Smith Papers Project

Published by the Church Historian's Press

An ongoing documentary editing project publishing every known document written by or to Joseph Smith, including journals, letters, revelations, and discourses. Primary sources, transcribed, annotated, and freely available online. This is the Church's own scholarship. If you want to know what the Prophet actually said and wrote, start here.

josephsmithpapers.org

Official | Church transparency initiative

Gospel Topics Essays

Published on ChurchofJesusChrist.org, 2013 to 2015

Thirteen essays commissioned under the direction of the Brethren to address specific historical questions: the Book of Abraham translation, polygamy in Nauvoo and Kirtland, race and the priesthood, the First Vision accounts, the nature of God, the Mountain Meadows Massacre, and others. Cited throughout this site. The essays are short, carefully worded, and worth reading in full.

churchofjesuschrist.org/gospel-topics-essays

Historical research | Primary-source ministry

Utah Lighthouse Ministry

Sandra Tanner (great-great-granddaughter of Brigham Young), Salt Lake City. Founded with her late husband Jerald in 1983.

Six decades of careful primary-source research on Mormon history, always citing the Church's own archives. Much of the evidence now acknowledged in the Gospel Topics essays was first put into wide circulation by the Tanners' work. A research resource; Sandra's theological framework is broadly trinitarian evangelical, but her historical scholarship is invaluable and remains the standard reference for documentary evidence on LDS history.

utlm.org
